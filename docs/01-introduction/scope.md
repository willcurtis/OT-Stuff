# Scope

This manual focuses on the technical aspects of designing, deploying, securing, and maintaining OT/BMS networks. It provides detailed operational guidance tailored to **network engineers** working with building automation systems.

---

## In Scope

### **1. BMS & OT Infrastructure**
- Controllers, sensors, field equipment  
- Panels, I/O modules, gateways  
- Supervisory platforms (BMS head-ends)

### **2. Industrial & BMS Protocols**
- BACnet/IP  
- BACnet MS/TP  
- Modbus TCP  
- OPC-UA  
- KNX  
- LonWorks  
- MQTT  

Including packet breakdowns and security considerations.

### **3. Network Architecture**
- VLAN segmentation  
- Zoning  
- Routing & switching  
- Redundancy and HA design  
- Firewalling and ACLs  
- Time synchronisation  

### **4. Security**
- Threat models  
- Zero Trust for OT  
- Protocol hardening  
- Secure remote access  
- OT DMZ design  

### **5. Deployment Patterns**
- Small sites  
- Campus networks  
- Retail/mall environments  
- Hospitals  
- Datacentres  

### **6. Troubleshooting**
- Layered fault analysis  
- Protocol-specific diagnosis  
- Field wiring and physical-layer issues  
- Traffic captures and interpretation  

### **7. Commissioning**
- Checklists  
- Acceptance testing  
- Addressing plans  
- BMS point list structures  

### **8. Templates & Examples**
- Real-world BACnet/IP design  
- BMS VPN/remote access pattern  
- Architecture diagrams  

---

## Out of Scope

- Mechanical/HVAC control theory  
- Low-level electrical installation methods  
- Vendor-specific proprietary training  
- Commercial/project management topics  

---

# Summary

This manual provides the level of detail required for modern OT/BMS network engineering while remaining practical, vendor-neutral, and technically rigorous.
